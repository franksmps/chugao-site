LED Driver Lifespan: MTBF, L70, and How Long They Really Last

Technical Deep-dive · April 2026 · 8 min read

A datasheet says "50,000 hours." You do the math: that is 5.7 years of continuous operation. So why do some installations need driver replacement after just 2-3 years?

The answer is that rated lifespan and real-world lifespan are different things. This article explains what those numbers actually mean, what kills drivers early, and how to pick the right spec for your project's expected service life.

The Three Metrics That Matter

MetricWhat it measuresTypical value
MTBFMean Time Between Failures — statistical average time between failures across a population50,000–100,000h
L70 / L80Hours until output drops to 70% or 80% of initial value30,000–50,000h
Warranty periodManufacturer guarantee against defects2–5 years
Key insight: A 50,000-hour L70 rating means the driver maintains at least 70% of its original output after 50,000 hours under test conditions. It does not mean every unit will run for 50,000 hours before failing.

Why Drivers Fail Early

1. Heat is the #1 killer

Every 10°C above the rated operating temperature roughly halves electrolytic capacitor life. An IP20 indoor driver mounted inside a sealed light fixture can easily run 20°C hotter than ambient. If the ambient is 35°C (common in summer), internal component temps can hit 75-85°C — well above the typical 60°C design point.

  • IP67 waterproof units: Better heat dissipation through metal case + silicone potting. Rated for -30 to +60°C. Typical lifespan: 50,000h.
  • IP20 indoor units: Depend on fixture ventilation. In sealed housings, expect 40-60% shorter life than rated.
  • Adapters: Plastic enclosure traps more heat. Typical lifespan: 30,000h.

2. Voltage spikes and surges

Grid voltage fluctuations (especially in developing markets) stress input capacitors and MOVs. A driver rated for AC 190-264V may survive a 280V transient once or twice but repeated surges degrade components faster than normal wear.

3. Running near full load

At 90-100% rated load, ripple current through output capacitors increases. This generates more heat and accelerates aging. The rule we recommend at CHUGAO:

Load wattage × 1.25 = minimum driver rating.
Running a driver at 70-80% capacity instead of 95% can extend effective lifespan by 30–50%.

Quick Decision Guide

  1. Daily runtime? 8h retail vs 24h industrial changes everything. Multiply your target project life by daily hours to get total hour requirement.
  2. Ambient temperature? Every 10°C over 40°C cuts life by ~50%. Factor this into model selection.
  3. Ventilation? Sealed fixtures need IP67 or higher. Ventilated enclosures can use IP20/65.
  4. Headroom? Always add 25%. The cost difference between a 60W and 100W driver is small compared to a site visit to replace a failed unit.
  5. Spare stock? For 24/7 installations, keep 5-10% spare drivers on hand. It costs less than emergency shipping.
