BIS Certification for LED Drivers: What India Importers Need to Know

Regulatory · May 2026 · 7 min read

Since 2018, India has progressively expanded its mandatory Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) registration requirement for electronic products. For anyone importing LED power supplies into India, BIS is no longer optional — it is a customs gate.

This guide explains what BIS means for your order, how the process works, why it matters for your timeline, and how working with a BIS-certified factory like CHUGAO saves you weeks of paperwork.

What Is BIS?

BIS is India's national standards body. Under the Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS), products in covered categories must carry a BIS registration mark before they can be imported or sold in India.

For LED switching power supplies, the applicable standard is IS 13252 (Part 1): Information Technology Equipment — Safety — General Requirements, which covers:

  • Electrical safety and insulation
  • Temperature rise limits
  • Protection against electric shock
  • Fire enclosure requirements
  • Component safety ratings
The key point: If your LED driver does not have BIS registration, Indian Customs can reject the shipment, delay clearance, or require re-export at your cost.

How the BIS Process Works

  1. Application: The manufacturer (or their authorized representative) submits an online application to BIS through the portal, along with technical documentation and test reports from a recognized lab.
  2. Factory inspection: BIS may audit the manufacturing facility to verify quality systems and production consistency.
  3. Testing: Samples are tested against IS 13252 (Part 1). This includes dielectric strength, temperature, humidity, and mechanical stress tests.
  4. Grant of license: Upon passing, BIS issues a registration number that appears on the product label and packaging.
  5. Surveillance: After registration, BIS conducts periodic follow-up audits to ensure ongoing compliance.

Why This Affects Your Timeline

If you are ordering LED drivers for the Indian market, you have two paths:

  • Path A — Order from a non-BIS factory: You handle BIS yourself. Expect 4-8 weeks of testing + application time before your goods can ship. Plus lab fees ($500-$2000 per model depending on complexity).
  • Path B — Order from a BIS-certified factory: The factory already holds the BIS license for the model series. Your order ships immediately. You receive the BIS certificate copy with the shipment documents.

CHUGAO holds BIS registration for our core LED driver models. When you place an India-bound order, we include the BIS certificate PDF in your shipping dossier — no extra waiting.

Practical Tips for India Buyers

  • Confirm BIS coverage early. Not all models may be registered. Ask us which SKUs have active BIS status before finalizing your SKU list.
  • Check HS code classification. LED power supplies typically fall under HS 8504.40 (electronic ballasts/converters). Confirm with your customs broker as rules change.
  • Allow 2-3 extra days for BIS document review at Indian ports. Even with correct papers, some ports do spot-checks on electronics shipments.
  • Label requirement: BIS-registered products must display the BIS Standard Mark on the unit and outer carton. We handle this labeling when you specify "India destination" on your order.
